In the first task, you will play {{Constants.num_rounds}} rounds of a search game.

In each round, you are going to search for an item to purchase. You start with no item on your searched list. You can choose to search for a new item at a constant cost of {{ search_cost }} points per search by clicking on Search button. With the current searched list, you can choose to purchase an item by entering the Search No. of the item that you want to purchase in the box and clicking on the Purchase button; or you may keep searching for the next new item at a constant cost of {{ search_cost }} points. The round ends when you make a purchase.

{% if control == True %}

The outcome of each item is an integer between {{ value_low }} points and {{ value_high }} points (inclusive). The items differ in the outcome (i.e. the points you will get). After each search, you will know the outcome of the item you just searched. The outcomes come with equal possibilities.

Suppose you have searched n times in this round, and the item you choose to purchase has the outcome of getting X points, then your payoff in this round is - n × {{ search_cost }} points + X points.

Example
Suppose you have searched 4 times and the item you choose to purchase shows Points You Will Get as 400 points, then your payoff is calculated as - 4 × {{ search_cost }} points + 400 points= 380 points.

Example
Suppose you have searched 4 times and the item you choose to purchase shows Points You Will Get as 180 points, then your payoff is calculated as - 4 × {{ search_cost }} points + 180 points = 160 points.

The outcome of each item is {{ value_high }} points × p + {{ value_low }} points × (1 - p), where p is the probability of getting {{ value_high }} points. The items differ in probability of getting {{ value_high }} points. Although p is defined as a probability, you will receive the outcome of the item (calculated above) you purchase for sure. After each search, you will know the p of the item you just searched.

Suppose you have searched n times in this round, and the item you choose to purchase has the probability of getting {{ value_high }} points as p, then your payoff in this round is - n × {{ search_cost }} points + {{ value_high }} points × p + {{ value_low }} points × (1 - p).

{% if value_high == 500 %}

Example
Suppose you have searched 4 times and the item you choose to purchase has Probability of Getting {{ value_high }} points as 0.75 (p = 0.75), then your payoff is calculated as - 4 × {{ search_cost }} points + {{ value_high }} points × 0.75 + {{ value_low }} points × (1 - 0.75) = 380 points.

For each item, there are two possible outcomes: {{ value_high }} points and {{ value_low }} points. The items differ in probability of getting {{ value_high }} points, which is defined as p. After each search, you will know the p of the item you just searched.

The outcome you receive from the item you purchase is either {{ value_high }} points or {{ value_low }} points, depending on the p of that item.

Suppose that you have searched n times in this round, then your payoff in this round is either - n × {{ search_cost }} points + {{ value_high }} points or - n × {{ search_cost }} points + {{ value_low }} points .

{% if value_high == 500 %}

Example
Suppose you have searched 4 times and the item you choose to purchase has Probability of Getting {{ value_high }} points as 0.75 (p = 0.75), then your payoff could be either - 4 × {{ search_cost }} points + {{ value_high }} points = 480 points with probability 0.75 or - 4 × {{ search_cost }} points + {{ value_low }} points = 80 points with probability 0.25.
